## Concept 1: Dynamic Glenoid Version
**Novelty: HIGH — concept unnamed in the literature**

### Search Results
- "dynamic glenoid version" OR "dynamic glenoid orientation" OR "functional glenoid version" → **0 results**
- Scapular dyskinesis + glenoid version/retroversion/orientation → **5 results** (clinical reviews, none study the concept)
- Scapular position/motion/kinematics + glenoid version/inclination (excl. arthroplasty) → **31 results**
- "scapulothoracic" + "glenoid version" → **6 results**

### Key Adjacent Papers
| Paper | Relevance |
|-------|-----------|
| **Bryce et al. 2010 (JBJS, 147 citations)** — "2D glenoid version measurements vary with coronal and sagittal scapular rotation" | **CLOSEST PAPER.** Proved the mechanics but framed as CT measurement artifact, not functional biomechanical concept. |
| **Akgun et al. 2024 (AJSM, 9 citations)** — Scapulothoracic orientation differences in posterior instability patients | Links scapular orientation to instability — conceptually adjacent |
| **Akgun et al. 2025 (JSES Int, 0 citations)** — Scapular morphology/orientation in OA patients vs. controls | Same group, extending to OA |
| **Stein et al. 2017 (J Hand Surg, 9 citations)** — Scapular position affects MRI-measured glenoid version | Measurement context, not functional |

### Assessment
The reframe from "measurement artifact" (Bryce 2010) to "functional biomechanical variable during living movement" is genuinely novel. Nobody has proposed that scapular dyskinesis dynamically alters effective glenoid version as a mechanism linking scapular dysfunction to glenoid wear patterns (Walch B0 → B1 → B2).

## Concept 2: Scapula-Sparing Implant Selection for rTSA
**Novelty: MODERATE — active field, but specific framework is new**

### Search Results
- Scapulothoracic function + reverse shoulder arthroplasty → **38 results** (active area)
- Scapular dyskinesis + reverse shoulder + outcome/predictor → **2 results** (essentially empty)
- SHR + implant design (lateralized/medialized) + reverse → **3 results**
- Pre-operative scapular function + shoulder surgery + outcome → **175 results** (broad, but none specific to dyskinesis predicting arthroplasty outcomes)

### Key Adjacent Papers
| Paper | Relevance |
|-------|-----------|
| **Moroder et al. 2020 (JSES, 48 citations)** — Posture and scapulothoracic orientation influence humeral component retrotorsion choice in rTSA | **CLOSEST PAPER.** Already proposes scapulothoracic orientation should influence component positioning. |
| **Moroder et al. 2022 (CORR, 38 citations)** — Patient posture affects simulated ROM in rTSA | Shows scapulothoracic position matters for planning |
| **Bauer et al. 2023 (JCM, 16 citations)** — "Challenges for Optimization of rTSA Part II: Subacromial Space, Scapular Posture, Moment Arms and Muscle Tensioning" | Explicitly addresses scapular posture in rTSA optimization |
| **Shekhbihi et al. 2025 (EFORT Open Reviews, 0 citations)** — "The scapulothoracic conundrum in reverse shoulder arthroplasty: where do we stand and what is yet to expand?" | Most topically aligned — frames this as an open problem |
| **Zdravkovic et al. 2020 (CORR, 17 citations)** — Scapulothoracic kinematics differ in RCA vs. normals | Pre-arthroplasty baseline kinematics |
| **Matsuki et al. 2021 (JSES Int, 13 citations)** — Good vs. poor elevation kinematics after rTSA | Scapulothoracic compensation predicts function |

### Assessment
Moroder, Bauer, and Shekhbihi are already publishing on scapulothoracic considerations in rTSA. However, **nobody has proposed**: (1) matching implant lateralization to pre-operative scapulothoracic reserve, (2) using pre-operative scapular dyskinesis to predict rTSA outcomes, or (3) a formal "scapula-sparing" implant selection framework. These specific contributions would be novel, but must cite and build on the existing work.

## Concept 3: Unifying Framework (CSA + Posterior Acromion + Dyskinesis + Arthroplasty)
**Novelty: HIGH — zero results connecting these streams**

### Search Results
- Scapula + "upstream" + shoulder + pathology → **0 results**
- "Critical shoulder angle" + scapular dyskinesis → **0 results**
- Scapular morphology + dyskinesis + review/framework/unifying → **0 results**
- "Posterior acromial" + "critical shoulder angle" → **10 results** (individual studies, no synthesis)
- Scapula + evolution + shoulder pathology → **55 results** (paleoanthropology, not clinical synthesis)

### Key Adjacent Papers
| Paper | Relevance |
|-------|-----------|
| **Passaplan et al. 2021 (JSES, 11 citations)** — CSA does NOT change over time | Supports CSA as risk factor, not consequence |
| **Verhaegen et al. 2021 (JSES, 15+17 citations)** — Statistical shape model for scapular anatomy in RCA and OA | Independent group using same approach as Gerber |
| **Ernstbrunner et al. 2020 (AJSM, 18 citations)** — Scapular morphology + cuff tear pattern independently predict pseudoparalysis | Gerber group — connects shape to clinical outcome |

### Assessment
Nobody has written a paper connecting CSA, posterior acromial morphology, scapular dyskinesis, and arthroplasty outcomes into a single framework. The Zurich group (Meyer, Ernstbrunner, Verhaegen) has the individual pieces but published them as separate empirical studies. A Current Concepts review synthesizing these streams would be the first of its kind.
