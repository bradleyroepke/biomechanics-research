## Figure 1: The Enthesis Gradient (Molecular Scale)

**Purpose:** Establish the foundational biological principle before discussing the shoulder. Show that tissue molecular composition continuously tracks the local mechanical environment within a single structure.

**Prompt:**

Create a high-resolution scientific illustration showing the graded transition zones at a tendon-to-bone insertion site (enthesis), suitable for publication in a peer-reviewed medical journal.

The illustration should show a longitudinal cross-section of a tendon inserting into bone, with four clearly labeled zones arranged left to right:

Zone 1 (far left): TENDON. Dense, parallel collagen fibers (Type I collagen). Elongated tenocytes aligned with fibers. Label: "Pure Tension." Color: light blue or white.

Zone 2 (left-center): UNMINERALIZED FIBROCARTILAGE. Fibers becoming less organized. Rounder cells (fibrocartilage cells). Type II collagen appearing. Proteoglycan content increasing. Label: "Transition: Tension + Compression." Color: gradual transition to light purple.

Zone 3 (right-center): MINERALIZED FIBROCARTILAGE. Mineral deposits visible. Type X collagen. Hypertrophic chondrocyte-like cells. A visible tidemark separating unmineralized from mineralized zones. Label: "Compression + Mineral." Color: gradual transition to darker purple.

Zone 4 (far right): BONE. Lamellar bone with osteocytes in lacunae. Label: "Pure Compression." Color: beige/tan.

Below the cross-section, include THREE aligned graphs spanning the same horizontal distance:

Graph 1: "Collagen Composition." Two curves. Type I collagen (blue line) starts high on the left (tendon) and decreases to near zero at bone. Type II collagen (green line) starts at zero on the left and increases through the fibrocartilage zones.

Graph 2: "Proteoglycan Content." Single curve (orange) starting low in tendon, rising through the fibrocartilage zones, reflecting increasing compressive loading.

Graph 3: "Tissue Stiffness (Young's Modulus)." Single curve (black) on a logarithmic y-axis showing the dramatic increase from tendon (~200 MPa) through fibrocartilage to bone (~20 GPa), spanning approximately two orders of magnitude.

At the very bottom, a single sentence caption: "At every point in the enthesis, molecular composition reflects the local mechanical environment. Tension-bearing zones contain Type I collagen. Compression-bearing zones contain Type II collagen and proteoglycans. The transition is continuous, not abrupt."

Style: Clean, professional scientific illustration. White background. Anatomically informed but schematic (not photorealistic). Consistent color palette. All labels in clean sans-serif font (Helvetica or Arial), minimum 8pt. The illustration should communicate that tissue composition is mechanically determined without requiring any additional text.

Dimensions: landscape, 7 inches wide by 5 inches tall, 300 DPI minimum.

## Figure 3: Competing Pathway Diagram (The Novel Contribution)

**Prompt:**

Create a high-resolution conceptual diagram for a peer-reviewed orthopedic journal showing how a single continuously varying morphological parameter (the critical shoulder angle) produces two diverging and mutually exclusive pathological pathways in the shoulder. This is the central novel concept of the paper.

Layout: the diagram flows from top to bottom with a central split.

TOP: A horizontal gradient bar representing the critical shoulder angle, ranging from 20 degrees on the far left to 45 degrees on the far right. The left end is colored deep blue, transitioning through neutral gray at the center (33 degrees, labeled "Normal") to deep red at the right end. Mark key thresholds: 28 degrees and 35 degrees with vertical dashed lines. Label the zones: less than 28 as "OA Risk Zone," 28 to 35 as "Transitional Zone," greater than 35 as "RCT Risk Zone."

From the left side of the gradient bar (blue zone), a pathway descends to the left:

LEFT PATHWAY (blue tones, flowing downward): A connected sequence of rounded boxes, each one step in the osteoarthritis cascade:
"Compression-dominant force environment" (arrow down to)
"Chronic articular cartilage overload" (arrow down to)
"Chondrocyte matrix adaptation, cartilage thinning" (arrow down to)
"Subchondral sclerosis" (arrow down to)
"Joint medialization" (arrow down to)
A large terminal box: "GLENOHUMERAL OSTEOARTHRITIS"

From the right side of the gradient bar (red zone), a pathway descends to the right:

RIGHT PATHWAY (red tones, flowing downward): A connected sequence of rounded boxes:
"Shear-dominant force environment" (arrow down to)
"Chronic supraspinatus overload" (arrow down to)
"Articular-side matrix remodeling" (arrow down to)
"Partial tearing at SS-IS junction" (arrow down to)
"Force couple disruption" (arrow down to)
"Superior migration, fatty infiltration" (arrow down to)
A large terminal box: "ROTATOR CUFF DISEASE"

THE CRITICAL NOVEL ELEMENT: Between the two pathways, draw two curved dashed arrows that cross from one pathway to the other, each with an X or stop symbol where it meets the opposite pathway:

Arrow 1: From "Joint medialization" on the left pathway, curving rightward with label: "Medialization shortens deltoid moment arm, reducing shear on cuff." It reaches the right pathway and is blocked by an X symbol.

Arrow 2: From "Superior migration" on the right pathway, curving leftward with label: "Superior migration unloads articular surface." It reaches the left pathway and is blocked by an X symbol.

These crossed arrows are the visual explanation of mutual exclusivity: each pathway's consequences geometrically prevent the other from developing.

CENTRAL ZONE: In the transitional zone between the two pathways (CSA 28 to 35), add a shaded region with small text: "Boundary zone: both pathways accessible. Outcome determined by tissue susceptibility, activity level, scapulothoracic function, and systemic health."

Caption at bottom: "Competing pathological pathways from a shared morphological parameter. The critical shoulder angle determines which tissue's mechanical threshold is crossed first. Each pathway's downstream geometric consequences alter the joint in ways that inhibit the other pathway, explaining the clinical observation that rotator cuff tears and glenohumeral osteoarthritis rarely coexist."

Style: Modern, clean infographic suitable for medical journal. White background. Blue tones for OA pathway, red tones for RCT pathway, neutral gray for transitional zone. Boxes with subtle rounded corners. Clean directional arrows. The crossing inhibition arrows should be visually prominent, perhaps in gold or dark gray with dashed styling to distinguish them from the pathway arrows. All text in clean sans-serif font, minimum 8pt at final reproduction size. The entire concept should be graspable in under 10 seconds of looking at the figure.

Dimensions: portrait, 6 inches wide by 8.5 inches tall, 300 DPI minimum.

## Figure 4: The Sarcomere Adaptation Story (Muscle Architecture Scale)

**Prompt:**

Create a high-resolution scientific illustration showing how rotator cuff muscle architecture progressively adapts after tendon tear, suitable for a peer-reviewed orthopedic journal. This figure demonstrates the framework's principle at the muscle fiber level.

The illustration should show four panels arranged left to right, representing progressive stages:

PANEL 1, labeled "Intact": A schematic muscle showing parallel fibers at a normal pennation angle of approximately 29 degrees relative to the tendon axis. Fibers are long and evenly spaced. Each fiber contains a chain of sarcomeres drawn as repeating units. The sarcomeres are at optimal length (approximately 2.7 micrometers, shown with a small scale bar). Color: healthy red/pink muscle fibers. Label the pennation angle clearly.

PANEL 2, labeled "Full-Thickness Tear (Adaptive)": The tendon is shown detached from bone. The muscle has retracted slightly. Fibers are shorter with slightly increased pennation angle (approximately 35 degrees). Sarcomere NUMBER has decreased (fewer units in series, shown visually) but individual sarcomere LENGTH remains near-normal. This represents adaptive sarcomere subtraction. Label: "Sarcomeres subtracted. Length maintained. Potentially reversible." Color: slightly paler muscle.

PANEL 3, labeled "Massive Tear (Maladaptive)": The tendon is significantly retracted. Fibers are much shorter with markedly increased pennation angle (approximately 55 degrees). Sarcomeres are both fewer in number AND shorter than optimal (approximately 2.6 micrometers). Yellow/white adipose tissue is visible between the muscle fibers (fatty infiltration). Label: "Sarcomeres shortened below optimal. Fatty infiltration. Function compromised." Color: pale muscle with yellow fat deposits interspersed.

PANEL 4, labeled "After Continuous Traction (Restored)": The muscle has been elongated back by slow continuous traction. Fibers are longer again. Pennation angle has returned to approximately 30 degrees. New sarcomeres have been added in series (more units in the chain, each at normal length). Some residual fatty infiltration remains between fibers but fiber architecture is normalized. Label: "Sarcomeres added in series. Architecture normalized. Fat arrested but not reversed." Color: healthier pink with some residual yellow.

Below all four panels, a horizontal arrow from left to right labeled "Time / Disease Progression" with a curved arrow from Panel 3 back toward Panel 4 labeled "Continuous mechanical traction (1 mm/day)."

Caption: "Progressive muscle architectural adaptation after rotator cuff tear. Full-thickness tears show adaptive sarcomere subtraction that maintains sarcomere length (potentially reversible). Massive tears show shortened sarcomeres and fatty infiltration (functionally compromised). Slow continuous mechanical traction normalizes fiber architecture and stimulates sarcomerogenesis, demonstrating that these changes represent mechanical adaptation rather than irreversible degeneration. Data from Gerber et al. and Gibbons et al."

Style: Clean scientific illustration. White background. Schematic (not photorealistic) but anatomically informed. Muscle fibers clearly drawn with visible internal sarcomere structure. Pennation angles clearly marked. Fat deposits visually distinct (yellow) from muscle (pink/red). Each panel should be the same size. Clean labels in sans-serif font. The progression from healthy to diseased to restored should be visually intuitive.

Dimensions: landscape, 8 inches wide by 4 inches tall, 300 DPI minimum.
