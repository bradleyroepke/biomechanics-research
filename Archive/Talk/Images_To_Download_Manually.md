### Tensegrity Structures (for Slide 13 — Closing)

1. **Snelson Needle Tower sculpture** — the most iconic tensegrity artwork
   - URL: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kenneth_Snelson (scroll to photos)
   - Save as: `WEB_01_Snelson_NeedleTower_iconic_tensegrity_sculpture.jpg`
   - WHY: The most recognizable tensegrity image in the world. 60-foot sculpture at the Hirshhorn Museum. Shows compression struts floating in tension cables at architectural scale.

2. **Simple tensegrity icosahedron model**
   - URL: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tensegrity (multiple model photos)
   - Save as: `WEB_02_tensegrity_icosahedron_simple_model.jpg`
   - WHY: Clean, simple desktop-scale model showing the principle. Easy for audience to understand. Levin proposed the icosahedron as the fundamental biological tensegrity unit.

3. **Tensegrity 3-strut prism** — the simplest possible tensegrity
   - URL: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tensegrity (look for the 3-strut model)
   - Save as: `WEB_03_tensegrity_3strut_simplest_model.jpg`
   - WHY: The minimum viable tensegrity — 3 struts, 9 cables. Shows the principle in its simplest form.

### Scapulothoracic Anatomy (for Slides 3-4)

4. **Scapulothoracic muscles — posterior view with all attachments labeled**
   - URL: https://www.kenhub.com/en/library/anatomy/scapulothoracic-joint
   - Save as: `WEB_04_scapulothoracic_muscles_posterior_labeled.jpg`
   - WHY: Clean anatomical illustration showing all muscles attaching to the scapula. The audience needs to see visually how many tension elements converge on this one bone.

5. **Scapulothoracic joint — showing the articulation with thorax**
   - URL: https://www.orthobullets.com/shoulder-and-elbow/3035/scapulothoracic-joint
   - Save as: `WEB_05_scapulothoracic_joint_thorax_relationship.jpg`
   - WHY: Shows the scapula's relationship to the rib cage — floating on the thorax with no bony articulation. Makes the "no bony stability" point visually.

6. **Scapular winging — clinical photograph**
   - URL: https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK541005/ (StatPearls - Winging of the Scapula)
   - Save as: `WEB_06_scapular_winging_clinical_photo.jpg`
   - WHY: Dramatic clinical photograph showing what happens when the tension network fails (long thoracic nerve palsy). The most vivid demonstration of the scapula's dependence on muscular stabilization.

### Evolutionary / Comparative Anatomy (for Slide 6)

12. **Primate scapular morphology comparison**
    - URL: Search Google Images for "hominoid scapula comparison evolution" or check Selby 2017 supplementary material
    - Save as: `WEB_12_primate_scapula_comparison_evolution.jpg`
    - WHY: Side-by-side scapulae across primate species showing the two fundamental changes (narrowing, glenoid migration). Visual proof of the evolutionary argument.

13. **Human vs quadruped scapular position**
    - URL: Search Google Images for "scapula position quadruped vs biped" or "scapula lateral vs posterior thorax evolution"
    - Save as: `WEB_13_scapula_position_quadruped_vs_biped.jpg`
    - WHY: Shows the scapula migrating from lateral (quadrupeds) to posterior (bipeds) thorax. The core of Selby 2017's argument.

### Muscle Architecture / Sarcomere (for Slide 12)

15. **Sarcomere length-tension curve**
    - URL: Search Google Images for "sarcomere length tension curve" — this is a standard physiology figure widely available
    - Save as: `WEB_15_sarcomere_length_tension_curve.jpg`
