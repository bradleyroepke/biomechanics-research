## CRITICAL CORRECTIONS TO OUR ASSUMPTIONS

### 1. The Garcia OR of 14.8 is fragile
- Based on only **8 full-thickness retears** out of 77 shoulders
- Confidence intervals were NOT reported in the paper — a red flag
- Should be cited as "preliminary" or "suggestive," never as definitive

### 2. The CSA-cuff tear link is NOT universally replicated
- **Bjarnison/Arnar 2017: OR = 1.12, p = .63 — NO association between CSA and RCT**
- Their RCT group mean CSA was 33.9° (vs 38° in Moor). Strikingly different.
- Methodological debates on both sides (tear size stratification, malrotation thresholds, ICD-10 coding)
- **The CSA-OA link IS universally replicated**, even by Bjarnison (OR 2.25, p = .002)

### 3. 48% of cuff tears occur below CSA 35°
- Mantell 2017: in patients with BOTH OA and RCT, 48% of cuff tears had CSA < 35°
- Sensitivity of the 35° cutoff is only 52%
- CSA is a risk modulator, not a deterministic threshold

### 4. CSA does not predict 24-month functional outcomes after repair
- Lee 2017: NO difference in CSS, OSS, or UCLA at 24 months between CSA groups
- Early (6-month) differences washed out by 2 years
- Caveat: no imaging to assess structural integrity; 24 months may be too short

### 5. Glenoid inclination may matter more than acromial extension
- Garcia 2017: GI alone (OR 15.0) was as predictive as CSA (OR 14.8) for retear
- Viehofer only modified acromial extension, not GI — so the FEA tested half the question

### 6. Reduced humeral retrotorsion is a major new variable
- Akgun 2024: C1 patients had 14.9° retrotorsion vs 34.4° controls (p < .001)
- Strong correlation with glenoid retroversion (r = -0.742)
- Not previously part of our framework

## WHAT THIS MEANS FOR THE FLAGSHIP PAPER

### Strengthen:
- The CSA-OA link is bulletproof — replicated even by skeptics
- CSA is congenital and stable (Passaplan, 12-year data)
- Dose-response for tear size (Blonna) strongly supports biomechanical gradient
- Posterior acromial morphology story is strong across 4 independent study designs
- Gerber 2025 SCOPE results (6/9 revision cases stabilized by bony correction alone) is powerful

### Must handle honestly:
- **Bjarnison's null finding for CSA-RCT cannot be dismissed.** Best response: tear-size stratification limitation, liberal malrotation threshold, ICD-10 coding. Present as "methodological debate," not "they were wrong."
- **Garcia's OR of 14.8 is based on n=8.** Cite as suggestive, not definitive.
- **48% of cuff tears below CSA 35°** (Mantell) — the threshold is probabilistic, not deterministic. This FITS the bifurcation model better than a binary cutoff (near the bifurcation zone, both pathways are accessible).
- **No 24-month outcome difference** (Lee) — frame as "CSA may influence rate of recovery more than endpoint" or note that 24 months may be too short for structural failure to manifest.

### New elements to incorporate:
- Glenoid inclination as potentially more important than acromial extension (Garcia)
- Humeral retrotorsion as a co-variable in posterior instability (Akgun) — not currently in framework
- GHSI vs SHSI dissociation — the decentering is a whole-scapula problem (Akgun)
- Reduced thoracic kyphosis in C1 patients (Akgun) — upstream postural variable
- Viehofer's deltoid-wrapping-on-SSP finding — the cuff as a passive pulley for deltoid

### Falsification-relevant data already present:

- Gerber 2018's finding that post-op CSA in healed vs failed repairs (33 vs 36°) supports the boundary-condition correction concept

## DEEP READ ROUND 2: Engler 2006, Frost 2003, Paszek 2005

### Engler 2006 (Cell) — Matrix Elasticity Directs Stem Cell Lineage

**What it proves:**
- Substrate stiffness alone (identical serum, same collagen-I coating) drives MSCs toward three distinct lineages: neurons on soft (0.1-1 kPa), muscle on medium (8-17 kPa), bone on stiff (25-40 kPa)
- Nonoverlapping optima for each lineage on the stiffness axis
- Blebbistatin (NMM II inhibitor) abolishes ALL stiffness-directed specification
- After 3 weeks on a given stiffness, cells COMMIT — chemical induction media for a different lineage cannot override the mechanically-directed fate

**What it doesn't prove:**
- Expression reaches only ~50% of committed control cells at 1 week — this is early specification, not terminal differentiation
- Full differentiation requires BOTH correct stiffness AND correct induction media — synergy, not mechanics alone
- At 1 week, chemistry CAN partially override mechanics (creating mixed phenotypes). Irreversibility only after 3 weeks
- 2D polyacrylamide gels, single ligand (collagen-I), sparse culture — distant from in vivo
- Population-level data — cannot fully distinguish instruction from selection

**Credence update for Claim 3:** ↑ moderate. Reformulated claim ("forces are a necessary co-determinant") is strongly supported. The synergy finding — full differentiation requires BOTH mechanics and chemistry — validates our "integrated mechanochemical system" framing over either primacy claim.

### Frost 2003 (Anat Rec) — Bone's Mechanostat: A 2003 Update

**Key thresholds (verified):**
- MESr (remodeling/disuse): 50-100 microstrain
- MESm (modeling/formation): 1,000-1,500 microstrain
- MESp (microdamage): ~3,000 microstrain
- Fx (fracture): ~25,000 microstrain

**Critical for the framework:**
- The mechanostat IS a threshold/bifurcation system — discrete regime changes switch qualitatively different biological programs
- Frost himself extends the concept to "fascia, joint, ligament, tendon, or growth plates" (Section 10) — threshold values unknown for non-bone tissues
- Disuse osteopenia framed as CORRECT mechanostat response, not disease: "physiologic osteopenias"
- Postmenopausal bone loss = mechanostat responding correctly to decreased muscle strength
- Therapeutic drug plateaus = mechanostat enforcing its set points against pharmacological intervention
- The thermostat analogy is explicit and perfect for the flagship paper

**Important caveats:**
- Diseased mechanostats exist (OI) — the regulatory system itself can be broken
- MES thresholds can be pathologically shifted by disease
- Nonmechanical agents (hormones, cytokines) are required "permissive agents" — not purely mechanical
- Stress fractures = genuine tissue failure from supraphysiologic damage, not adaptive response

**Credence update for Claim 4:** ↑ strong for bone specifically. The mechanostat framework directly supports "correct response to altered information" for disuse osteopenia, postmenopausal bone loss, and stress shielding. Qualifier needed: "in systems with intact mechanostats."

### Paszek 2005 (Cancer Cell) — Tensional Homeostasis and Malignant Phenotype

**The finding:** Normal mammary epithelial cells with NO genetic mutations exhibit malignant behavior (proliferation, loss of polarity, disrupted architecture) when placed on matrix matching tumor stiffness (~4000 Pa vs normal ~170 Pa). Conversely, genetically malignant cells REVERT toward normal behavior when Rho/ROCK/myosin is inhibited.

**Key numbers:**
- Normal mammary gland: 167 ± 31 Pa
- Tumors: 4,049 ± 938 Pa (24x stiffer)
- Tissue culture plastic: 2.78 × 10⁹ Pa (17 MILLION times stiffer than normal tissue)

**The V737N experiment (decisive):** Cells with integrins engineered to self-cluster (mimicking stiff matrix) show malignant phenotype ON COMPLIANT GELS. No oncogenic mutations. The integrins "think" the matrix is stiff → the cell responds with proliferation and loss of polarity.

**The positive feedback loop:** Stiff matrix → integrin clustering → FA assembly → ERK → Rho → ROCK → more contractility → more matrix stiffening. Each step is normal mechanotransduction. Cancer arises when this loop becomes self-sustaining.

**The tissue culture plastic insight:** Virtually all cancer biology has been done on plastic 17 million times stiffer than normal breast tissue. Cells on plastic inevitably show elevated Rho, ERK, proliferation. "Malignant behavior" in culture may partly be normal cells responding to a grotesquely abnormal mechanical environment.

**Caveats:**
- Oncogenes can eventually bypass mechanics (ERK becomes constitutively active)
- T4-2 malignant cells generate their own aberrant mechanical signal (elevated Rho without stiff matrix) — inside-out, not just outside-in
- Temporal sequence in vivo unclear: does stromal stiffening precede or follow early neoplasia?
- Phenotypic reversion is not cure — genetic alterations remain
- All experiments in soft tissue (breast); extension to musculoskeletal tissues not tested

**This is independent of Ingber.** Weaver lab (UPenn), no shared authors, minimal cross-citation. Convergence of two independent programs on the same conclusion.

**Credence update for Claim 4:** ↑ strong. This is the strongest independent evidence that "pathology is correct response to altered mechanical information." The V737N experiment is particularly powerful — no mutations, just mechanical misinformation, and the cell responds with pathological behavior.
