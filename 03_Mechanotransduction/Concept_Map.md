# Complete Concept Map: From First Principles to Clinical Application

*Every concept at our disposal, organized by level. Concepts marked [IN PAPER] are in the current manuscript draft. All others are available but not yet used.*

## LEVEL 1: FUNDAMENTAL PHYSICS AND UNIVERSAL PRINCIPLES

These are laws and principles that apply to ALL physical systems, not just biology. They form the deepest foundation.

### In the current paper:
- [IN PAPER] Threshold/set-point behavior (Frost's mechanostat as a thermostat analogy)

### Available but not in the paper:
- **Constructal Law** (Bejan 1996): Any flow system spontaneously evolves toward configurations minimizing resistance. Wolff's Law is a special case. Trabecular alignment = Constructal Law on a solid mechanical system.
- **Hormesis**: The mechanical dose-response IS a hormesis curve. Too little = atrophy. Optimal = adaptation. Too much = damage. Complete protection removes the hormetic stimulus maintaining the adaptive machinery.
- **Sensitive dependence on initial conditions** (chaos theory): Morphological parameters are initial conditions of a nonlinear system. Small differences at skeletal maturity produce exponentially diverging trajectories over decades. Explains why small CSA differences predict large outcome differences.
- **Dissipative structures** (Prigogine): Living tissue is a far-from-equilibrium system maintained by continuous energy throughput. Degeneration is the thermodynamic default. Maintenance requires continuous work. Immobilization removes the input. This is physics, not pathology.
- **Shannon information theory**: Monotonous loading = low-entropy signal, cells accommodate. Variable loading = high-entropy signal, maintains information content. Rehabilitation that varies parameters is informationally superior.
- **Self-organized criticality** (Per Bak): Healthy tissue may operate at a critical state, maximally sensitive to perturbation. Pathology = departure from criticality.
- **Allometric scaling** (West): Biological systems scale with quarter-power laws across 27 orders of magnitude. Morphological parameters exist within mathematical constraint envelopes.
- **Conservation of energy**: Forces applied to a system must be distributed throughout. Cannot be created or destroyed, only redirected.
- **Stress concentration** (materials science): A discontinuity (tear, notch) amplifies local stress far beyond average. Tear propagation is an inevitable mechanical consequence of stress risers, not a healing failure.
- **Fatigue failure** (materials science): Each individual loading event is tolerated, but cumulative cycles progressively degrade material resistance. Cuff degeneration is fatigue failure of a biological material with finite fatigue life.

## LEVEL 2: BIOLOGICAL PRINCIPLES (Tissue-General)

These apply to all living tissues, not specific to any joint.

### In the current paper:
- [IN PAPER] Wolff's Law (bone remodels along stress lines)
- [IN PAPER] Frost's mechanostat (strain thresholds: 50-100 / 1,000-1,500 / 3,000 microstrain)
- [IN PAPER] Hueter-Volkmann (compression inhibits growth, tension stimulates; 17% per 0.1 MPa)
- [IN PAPER] Davis's Law (soft tissue remodels along imposed demand)
- [IN PAPER] Stress shielding (bone resorbs when load is bypassed)
- [IN PAPER] Cartilage loading (requires cyclic load; immobilize = degrade)
- [IN PAPER] Mechanotransduction via integrins (force to genome in milliseconds)
- [IN PAPER] Engler 2006 (substrate stiffness directs stem cell lineage; irreversible at 3 weeks)
- [IN PAPER] Frost's extension to non-bone tissues ("fascia, joint, ligament, tendon, or growth plates")

### Available but not in the paper:
- **Piezoelectric signaling**: Mechanical deformation of bone generates electrical potentials driving osteoblast activity. Literal force-to-signal conversion.
- **Titin as master sarcomere mechanosensor**: Third myofilament spanning Z-disc to M-line. Kinase domains mechanically activated by stretch. Upregulates mTORC1 protein synthesis independent of neural activation. The sarcomere senses its own length.
- **Sarcomerogenesis vs hypertrophy as distinct pathways**: Hypertrophy = parallel addition via FAK-PI3K-Akt-mTORC1 (active contraction). Sarcomerogenesis = serial addition via titin kinase (passive stretch on descending limb). Different stimuli, different pathways, different architectural outcomes.
- **The length-tension operating point model**: All muscle pathology maps to sarcomere position. Plateau = homeostasis. Ascending limb (short) = atrophy, sarcomere loss, fatty infiltration. Descending limb (long) = sarcomerogenesis if within titin range, injury if beyond.
- **YAP/TAZ as the mechanical sensor**: Nuclear YAP drives anabolism. Cytoplasmic YAP = atrophy. Supraphysiologic strain dysregulates YAP. Dose-response is inverted U, not monotonic.
- **Ligamentization**: Hamstring graft transforms into ligament-like tissue in the knee. Same cells, same genome, different mechanical environment = different tissue phenotype. Engler at the organ scale.
- **CA ligament regeneration**: Resected CA ligament regrows histologically normal (Levy 2001). The body rebuilds structures when the mechanical demand persists.
- **Cook's tendinopathy continuum**: Reactive (reversible) to dysrepair to degenerative. Each stage is an adaptation to loading.
- **Compressive tendinopathy**: Compression against bony prominences drives fibrocartilaginous metaplasia. Aggrecan 7-17x higher in compressed vs tensioned regions of same tendon.
- **Regional molecular encoding**: Tissue composition tracks mechanical environment at every point. Type I collagen in tension zones, Type II + aggrecan in compression zones (Achilles enthesis, supraspinatus, intervertebral disc).
- **Thresholds are not fixed**: They shift with loading history (progressive loading raises ceiling), repair state (healing tissue has lower threshold), systemic biology (diabetes lowers thresholds), prior injury (disrupted ECM distorts signal).
- **The genetics-mechanics feedback loop**: Over evolutionary time, mechanics shaped genetics. Over developmental time, genetics shapes mechanics. Over a lifetime, mechanics shapes gene expression. Over a disease course, changed expression changes mechanics. They are reciprocal, not competing.
- **Heritability of cuff disease**: 18% genetic, 44% shared environment, 38% unique environment. Siblings 2.85x risk. Specific variants identified (DEFB1, FGFR1, RUNX1, ADAM12, TGFBR2).

## LEVEL 3: SHOULDER-SPECIFIC BIOMECHANICS

### In the current paper:
- [IN PAPER] CSA determines deltoid force vector direction
- [IN PAPER] Low CSA = compression-dominant = OA pathway
- [IN PAPER] High CSA = shear-dominant = RCT pathway
- [IN PAPER] Beeler: acromial roof extension (d=1.36) > glenoid inclination (d=0.60)
- [IN PAPER] Viehofer: 5° CSA change = 8.5% JRF change; compression-to-shear ratio ~1.8:1
- [IN PAPER] Parsons: SS tear = no force change; SS+IS = 61% drop + 15° shift (THE threshold)
- [IN PAPER] Burkhart: functional vs dysfunctional tears = threshold of force couple integrity
- [IN PAPER] Hashimoto: 80 specimens, 100% degeneration, 0% inflammation, articular > bursal
- [IN PAPER] Kim: tear initiation at 13-17mm posterior to biceps (SS/IS junction)
- [IN PAPER] Melis: moderate FI at 4 years, severe at 6 years
- [IN PAPER] Ward: SSP work capacity 4.0 vs SSC 15.1; shortest fibers, longest sarcomere resting length
- [IN PAPER] Gerber traction: pennation normalized (55° to 29.8°), sarcomerogenesis, FI arrested
- [IN PAPER] Competing pathway mutual exclusivity (medialization reduces shear; superior migration unloads cartilage)
- [IN PAPER] Mantell: dual pathology at CSA ~35° (boundary zone)
- [IN PAPER] Bjarnison dissent: no CSA-RCT association (OR 1.12, p=.63)
- [IN PAPER] Meta-analysis heterogeneity (I²=93%)
- [IN PAPER] Measurement reliability (76% inadequate radiographs)
- [IN PAPER] Passaplan: CSA stable over 12 years
- [IN PAPER] Blonna: dose-response (isolated SSP 36°, large tears 40°, OA 28°)

### Available but not in the paper:
- **Greater tuberosity angle**: Large GT angle = bursal tears. Large CSA = articular tears. Two morphological parameters driving two tear types at two surfaces of the same tendon.
- **Saha 1973**: First to quantify glenoid inclination as shear-to-compression determinant. Anticipated the CSA concept by 40 decades.
- **Nyffeler 2004**: 5mm COR shift converts abductor to adductor. The most dramatic phase transition in shoulder biomechanics.
- **Nyffeler 2006**: 0.5mm humeral displacement per degree of glenoid version. Humeral version cannot compensate. Scapula is the master regulator.
- **Lippitt & Matsen 1993**: Stability ratio = 10.3 x (effective depth) + 9.7. Linear relationship between glenoid morphology and stability.
- **Gerber 1987**: Scapular morphology explains 69% of variance in coracohumeral impingement space.
- **Buchler 2002 FEA**: Self-reinforcing loop between version and loading in OA. Posterior loading causes erosion causes more retroversion causes more posterior loading.
- **Rathbun 1970**: Vascular "wringing out" is position-dependent, not fixed. Scapular position modulates tendon vascularity.
- **Associated pathology clustering**: High-CSA phenotype clusters with biceps pathology, CA ligament changes, AC arthritis, labral tears. These aren't independent comorbidities; they're cascading downstream consequences.
- **CA ligament**: Unique (connects two parts of same bone), shared with hominoids only, regenerates after resection, functions as anterosuperior restraint. The framework in one structure.
- **Stress concentration at tear edge**: A partial tear is a stress riser. Local stress at the margin exceeds threshold even when global tendon stress is safe. Progressive propagation is mechanics, not failed healing.
- **Fatigue failure model for cumulative degeneration**: Each loading cycle is tolerated. Millions of cycles degrade material resistance. Not pathology. Physics operating on a biological material.
- **The "tragedy of local rationality"**: Every tissue does the right thing locally (chondroid metaplasia, fatty infiltration, acromial spurs, cable hypertrophy). The sum of those right things is catastrophe.
- **Keener natural history**: 49% of FT tears enlarge at 5 years. No tears heal. Cable disruption predicts progression. Median time from enlargement to FI = 1 year.
- **Craik 2013**: Human supraspinatus fossa SMALLER than other apes. Structural insufficiency, not impingement.
- **Kuhn 2016**: CA ligament exists because of throwing evolution. Depressed greater tuberosity allows extreme ER for throwing but creates cuff vulnerability.
- **Gibbons 2016**: FTT maintains sarcomere length through adaptive subtraction. MT has shortened sarcomeres below optimal. Predicted post-repair sarcomere length for MT = 8.46 micrometers (extreme overstrain).

## LEVEL 4: POSTERIOR INSTABILITY AND GLENOID MORPHOLOGY

### In the current paper:
- [IN PAPER] Meyer: PAH ≥23mm = OR 39 for posterior instability
- [IN PAPER] Hochreiter: high flat acromion reduces posterior stability force by 23-60%
- [IN PAPER] Gerber SCOPE: 8/9 stabilized, 6 were revisions
- [IN PAPER] Dynamic glenoid version (named concept, zero Scopus results)
- [IN PAPER] Bryce: scapular rotation changes version by up to 22.5°
- [IN PAPER] Akgun: C1 patients have half normal scapular tilt, less kyphosis

### Available but not in the paper:
- **Akgun: reduced humeral retrotorsion** (14.9° vs 34.4°) in C1 patients. Major variable not in our framework.
- **Walch B0 to B1 to B2 progression**: Subluxation precedes erosion. Positive feedback: retroversion causes posterior loading causes erosion causes more retroversion.
- **GHSI vs SHSI dissociation** (Akgun): Head appears centered on glenoid face but decentered relative to scapular body axis. The problem is whole-scapula malorientation.
- **Domos 2017**: B0 glenoid is the "shoulder at risk." Only 5° additional retroversion significantly increases posterior translation.

## LEVEL 5: REVERSE TSA AND SCAPULOTHORACIC DEPENDENCE

### In the current paper:
- [IN PAPER] Walker: SHR drops to 1.3:1 after rTSA
- [IN PAPER] Kawashima: SHR ≥2 = ASES 94.3 vs 82.1
- [IN PAPER] Mercurio: scapular dyskinesis persists after successful cuff repair

### Available but not in the paper:
- **Boileau 2005**: Grammont design medializes COR by 19mm, recruits deltoid segments I-IV. A deliberate redesign of shoulder morphology to create a new mechanical state.
- **Moroder 2020**: Scapular internal rotation correlates with needed humeral retrotorsion at R=0.91. Ignoring scapulothoracic posture causes predictable implant mismatch.
- **Ward 2006**: Total cuff PCSA lost in CTA = 36.07 cm². Deltoid becomes sole motor. Scapula becomes sole load-bearing platform.
- **Sirveaux 2004**: 63.6% scapular notching. Survivorship 29.8% at 8 years. The scapula is being overloaded.
- **Nyffeler 2005**: 2-4mm glenosphere repositioning changes adduction deficit from -25° to -1°. Scapular neck geometry is the rate-limiting factor.
- **Bauer 2022**: Contradiction between Moroder simulations (posture matters) and Reintgen clinical data (kyphosis didn't affect outcomes). Unresolved.
- **Grammont's evolutionary reasoning** (Baulot 2011): Evolution reduced supraspinatus while lateralizing acromion. Reverse prosthesis compensates for this evolutionary force imbalance.

## LEVEL 6: CROSS-JOINT UNIVERSALITY

### In the current paper:
- [IN PAPER] Prediction 6 (lateral epicondylitis, extending beyond shoulder)

### Available but not in the paper:
- **Spine: Pelvic incidence predicts degeneration**. PI-LL mismatch = adjacent segment disease (OR 10.6). Different PI = degeneration at different levels. High PI + shear at lumbosacral junction = spondylolisthesis.
- **Knee: Posterior tibial slope predicts ACL tears**. 12% increased risk per degree. Also predicts graft failure after reconstruction.
- **Hip: Femoral morphology predicts OA**. Coxa valga + shallow acetabulum = laterally directed forces = OA.
- **FAI as elegant demonstration**: Cam morphology (alpha angle) produces spatially specific anterosuperior damage exactly where mechanical model predicts. Spatial specificity = strongest evidence for mechanically-determined tissue fate.
- **HTO cartilage regeneration**: Mechanical realignment alone produces cartilage regeneration without biological augmentation. Adding microfracture/BMAC/stem cells did NOT improve over HTO alone. The biology was adequate; the mechanics was the limiting factor.
- **Intervertebral disc**: Type I collagen in outer annulus (tension), gradual transition to Type II + aggrecan in nucleus (compression). Molecular composition at every point tracks local mechanical environment within a single organ.
- **Meniscus**: Outer cells stiffer/ligament-like (tension zone), inner cells softer/chondrocyte-like (compression zone). 12 weeks training increases calcium, collagen, proteoglycan.
- **Achilles enthesis organ**: Three distinct fibrocartilages, each matching local mechanical environment. Sesamoid fibrocartilage formed by metaplasia in response to compression against calcaneus.
- **Calcaneal spurs**: Bone growing in the direction of the tensile vector. Wolff's Law made visible.
- **Heterotopic ossification**: Progenitor cells reading altered substrate stiffness, committing to osteogenic fate per Engler. Bone in the wrong place, but cells doing the right thing.
- **Ligamentization**: ACL graft transforms from tendon to ligament phenotype based on mechanical environment alone.
- **Proposed cross-joint study**: Measure PTS and CSA in same patients to test whether morphological parameters cluster within individuals.

## LEVEL 7: CLINICAL APPLICATIONS AND INTERVENTIONS

### In the current paper:
- [IN PAPER] Prediction 1: dual pathology clusters at CSA 30-35°
- [IN PAPER] Prediction 2: lateral acromioplasty + repair should reduce retear
- [IN PAPER] Prediction 3: over-correction below 28° should increase OA risk (most novel)
- [IN PAPER] Prediction 4: scapulothoracic posture predicts B0 to B2 progression
- [IN PAPER] Prediction 5: pre-op kinematics predict rTSA outcomes
- [IN PAPER] Prediction 6: compression-modified rehab for lateral epicondylitis
- [IN PAPER] Falsification criterion stated explicitly

### Available but not in the paper:
- **Prevention through scapulothoracic optimization**: Screen high-risk morphology + scapular dysfunction. Intervene before symptoms. Widen margin between current loading and threshold.
- **Rehabilitation as information restoration**: Not just strengthening muscles but restoring correct mechanical information to tissue. Scapulothoracic focus, not just cuff strengthening.
- **Regeneten reinterpretation**: Works as mechanical environment modifier (substrate stiffness, strain protection, load distribution), not primarily bioinductive. Any scaffold with similar stiffness should work regardless of biological composition.
- **Tensionless repair rationale**: Tension places enthesis cells above optimal strain window from day 1. Tensionless allows cells in anabolic window (4-8% strain). Progressive load transfer as matrix matures.
- **Post-RCR stiffness as protective**: Capsular stiffness distributes load away from repair, range-gates strain environment, and TGF-beta driving fibrosis also drives repair matrix deposition. Same biological response producing "complication" and healing.
- **Phase-dependent optimal mechanical environment**: Weeks 0-2 protect (extrinsic healing). Weeks 3-12 optimize loading (intrinsic healing dominant).
- **Molecular archaeology**: Biopsy articular vs bursal surface from same patient during repair. Predict distinct mechanically-encoded proteomic signatures. Spatial transcriptomics of supraspinatus not yet done.
- **The prospective morphological screening study**: Musculoskeletal Framingham. Radiograph at 25 predicts disease at 45. Changes preventive medicine for the entire musculoskeletal system.
- **Sarcomerogenesis-targeted rehabilitation**: If architectural problem requires serial sarcomere addition (not hypertrophy), rehab should target the titin/passive stretch pathway, not the standard resistance/hypertrophy pathway.

## LEVEL 8: BOUNDARIES AND HONEST LIMITS

### In the current paper:
- [IN PAPER] Bjarnison dissent engaged
- [IN PAPER] Garcia OR 14.8 flagged as fragile (n=8)
- [IN PAPER] No RCT for CSA correction
- [IN PAPER] 76% measurement reliability problem
- [IN PAPER] Framework doesn't apply to trauma, inflammatory, infectious, genetic
- [IN PAPER] Ascertainment bias caveat for mutual exclusivity
- [IN PAPER] Genetic heritability acknowledged
- [IN PAPER] Falsification criterion explicit

### Available but not in the paper:
- **RA hits small joints first**: Not the most loaded joints. Mechanics modifies pattern but doesn't drive it.
- **Teleological fallacy risk**: Calling adaptation "rational" infers purpose. Evolution doesn't optimize for joint longevity past reproduction. Better language: "mechanically-determined cell fate."
- **Unfalsifiability risk**: If every response can be labeled adaptive, the framework explains everything and predicts nothing. Must have clear falsification criteria (already addressed).
- **Meta-analysis I²=93%**: The CSA-RCT effect may be weaker than individual studies suggest.
- **The "genetics with extra steps" challenge**: If genetics determines both morphology AND tissue quality, the mechanical layer may add no independent explanatory power. Only resolved by interventional data.
- **Subchondral cysts**: Possibly genuinely maladaptive with no compensatory function. Challenges the universal adaptation claim.
- **De Quervain's postpartum**: Can develop without mechanical provocation (hormonal trigger). Defines boundary.
- **Dupuytren's**: Progression is mechanical but initiation appears chemical/genetic.
- **Point of no return**: Probably at complete structural discontinuity or total fibrotic replacement. Clinical point of no return may be earlier than biological one because we lack tools to deliver the right signal. "Not a biological limitation. An engineering limitation."
