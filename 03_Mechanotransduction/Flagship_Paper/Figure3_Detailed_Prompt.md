# Figure 3: Competing Pathway Diagram — Detailed Generation Prompt

## Overall Description
A high-resolution scientific infographic for publication in a peer-reviewed orthopedic surgery journal. The diagram illustrates how a single morphological parameter (the critical shoulder angle) produces two diverging and mutually exclusive pathological pathways in the glenohumeral joint. The visual style should match the quality of figures published in the Journal of Shoulder and Elbow Surgery or the Journal of Bone and Joint Surgery. The figure must be immediately comprehensible to an orthopedic surgeon without reading any accompanying text.

## Canvas and Layout
- Dimensions: exactly 6 inches wide by 9 inches tall
- Resolution: 300 DPI (1800 x 2700 pixels)
- Background: pure white (#FFFFFF)
- Overall layout: vertically oriented, flowing from top to bottom
- The figure divides into three horizontal zones: a top zone (the morphological parameter bar), a middle zone (the two diverging pathways), and a bottom zone (the crossing inhibition arrows explaining mutual exclusivity)
- All elements are centered on the vertical midline of the canvas
- Generous white space between elements for visual breathing room

## Zone 3: The Crossing Inhibition Arrows (Bottom 25% of canvas)

This is the most important and novel visual element. It explains WHY the two conditions are mutually exclusive.

From the left pathway's "Joint medialization" box (box 5), draw a curved dashed arrow that sweeps rightward in a smooth arc, crossing the center of the diagram and reaching the right pathway at approximately the level of boxes 2-3. Where the arrow meets the right pathway, place a bold red X symbol or a circular prohibition sign (circle with diagonal line through it), 0.3 inches in diameter. Along the arc of the arrow, place a text label in 7.5pt Helvetica italic, dark gray:

"Medialization reduces deltoid moment arm → decreased shear on cuff"

From the right pathway's "Superior migration" component of box 6, draw a curved dashed arrow that sweeps leftward in a smooth arc, crossing the center and reaching the left pathway at approximately the level of boxes 2-3. Where it meets the left pathway, place a bold blue X symbol or prohibition sign, same size. Along this arc, label:

"Superior migration unloads articular surface → decreased compression on cartilage"

These two crossing arrows should form a visual X pattern between the pathways, clearly communicating that each pathway's consequences block the other. The arrows should be dashed (4pt dash, 3pt gap), colored dark gold (#8B7D3C) or charcoal (#444444) to distinguish them from the pathway arrows. The X/prohibition symbols should be bold and immediately visible.

## Caption (Below the figure, outside the main canvas)

In 8.5pt Helvetica, dark charcoal, full width:

"Figure 3. Competing pathological pathways from a shared morphological parameter. The critical shoulder angle determines the ratio of compressive to shear force at the glenohumeral joint. A low angle favors compression and drives the osteoarthritis pathway (left, blue). A high angle favors shear and drives the rotator cuff disease pathway (right, red). Critically, each pathway's downstream geometric consequences inhibit the other: medialization in osteoarthritis reduces shear on the cuff, while superior migration after cuff tear unloads the articular surface. This geometric feedback explains why the two conditions rarely coexist. In the boundary zone (CSA 30-35°), both pathways are accessible and other patient-specific factors determine which threshold is crossed first."

## Style Specifications
- No drop shadows on boxes
- No 3D effects
- No photorealistic elements
- Clean, flat design throughout
- Consistent 0.1-inch corner radius on all rounded rectangles
- Arrow connectors: simple straight or gently curved lines with filled triangular heads
- Color palette restricted to the blue and red families specified above, plus neutral grays and the gold/charcoal for crossing arrows
- All text anti-aliased and crisp
- The overall impression should be: authoritative, clean, modern, scientific. Similar in quality to figures in Nature Reviews or The Lancet infographics.
