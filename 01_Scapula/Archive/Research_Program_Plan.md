# Research Program Plan
## The Scapula as an Upstream Determinant of Shoulder Pathology
### Brad Roepke, MD
### Living Document — March 2026

---

## Vision

To build the empirical evidence base for understanding the scapulothoracic articulation as a primary determinant of shoulder function and pathology, through a systematic research program that begins with studies achievable by a single clinician-investigator and progressively expands to collaborative computational and prospective work.

## Intellectual Context

This research program builds on the work of several groups who have independently identified the scapula as a significant variable in shoulder pathology but have not unified their findings into a single framework:

- **Christian Gerber and the Zurich group:** CSA (Moor 2013), posterior acromial morphology (Meyer 2019), biomechanical validation (Viehöfer 2016, Gerber 2014, Hochreiter 2023), lateral acromioplasty (Gerber 2018), SCOPE osteotomies (Gerber 2025), CSA temporal stability (Passaplan 2021)
- **Ben Kibler:** Kinetic chain concept (1998), scapular dyskinesis classification, SICK scapula (with Burkhart and Morgan 2003), scapular role in athletic function, the 51%/54% energy data
- **Philipp Moroder and the Berlin group:** Scapulothoracic orientation in rTSA planning (2020, 2022), posture types A-C, scapulothoracic orientation in posterior instability (Akgun 2024, 2025)
- **Paula Ludewig:** 3D scapular kinematics in impingement (2000), multiplanar humeral elevation (2009), the foundational kinematic data
- **Walker/Wright/Banks (Florida):** Scapulohumeral rhythm after rTSA (2015), 3D fluoroscopic measurement
- **Kawashima:** SHR correlated with rTSA outcomes (2025)
- **Donald Ingber:** Cellular tensegrity (1993, 1997, 2003, 2014), mechanotransduction
- **Jed Kuhn:** Evolutionary framework for the shoulder (2016)

What has not been done is connecting these streams into a unified framework with testable predictions. That is the contribution of the perspective paper. The research program below is designed to test whether the framework holds up empirically.

## Core Assumptions (Evaluated)

1. **The universe operates through physical principles including tensile and compressive forces.** Confidence: Absolute. Not debatable.

2. **Evolution selects for metabolically efficient solutions, subordinate to reproductive fitness.** Confidence: High. Caveat: efficiency is traded for functional advantage when needed (depressed tuberosity).

3. **The human shoulder was significantly shaped by the ability to throw.** Confidence: Moderate-high. Supported by Kuhn 2016, Roach 2013 (Nature). Caveat: throwing was one of several selection pressures, not the only one.

4. **Structures conserved across evolutionary scales may reflect optimized solutions to common physical problems.** Confidence: High for the general principle. Moderate for tensegrity specifically being conserved vs. convergent at the musculoskeletal level.

5. **The scapulothoracic articulation functions as a tension-dependent system whose morphology and position determine downstream force distribution.** Confidence: High. Supported by CSA data, posterior acromial morphology data, kinematic data, and rTSA data. This is the central testable claim.

## TIER 1: Studies I Can Do Alone (Residency / Early Fellowship)

### Study 1: CSA and Retear at My Institution
- **Design:** Retrospective chart review
- **Population:** All arthroscopic rotator cuff repairs at my institution, past 3-5 years
- **Measurements:** Pre-operative CSA on AP radiograph (30 sec/film), retear status from follow-up
- **Analysis:** Correlation between CSA and retear rate. Replicate Garcia (OR 14.8 for CSA >38)
- **Requirements:** IRB approval. No funding, no equipment, no collaborators.
- **Timeline:** Data collection 2-4 weekends. Analysis and manuscript 1 month.
- **Why this first:** Generates original data from my institution. Tests a core framework component. Publishable regardless of outcome. Builds credibility for approaching collaborators.
- **Status:** Not started

### Study 2: CSA Distribution in B-Type vs Concentric OA Glenoids
- **Design:** Retrospective radiographic study
- **Population:** All shoulder arthroplasty patients at my institution with pre-op AP radiographs and CT
- **Measurements:** CSA on AP radiograph. Walch glenoid classification from CT.
- **Analysis:** Compare CSA distribution between B-type (B1/B2) and concentric OA (A1/A2)
- **Question:** Do B-type glenoid patients have different scapular morphology than concentric OA patients?
- **Requirements:** IRB approval. Access to radiology database.
- **Timeline:** 1-2 months
- **Status:** Not started

### Study 3: Scapulothoracic Orientation in B-Type Glenoid Patients
- **Design:** Retrospective CT measurement study
- **Population:** Same as Study 2
- **Measurements:** 3D scapulothoracic orientation parameters (Akgun 2024 methods) from pre-op CTs
- **Analysis:** Compare scapulothoracic orientation between B-type and concentric OA groups
- **Question:** Do B-type patients have different scapulothoracic orientation? Does this replicate Akgun?
- **Requirements:** Learning the Akgun measurement technique from the published methods. May need 3D reconstruction software.
- **Timeline:** 2-3 months
- **Status:** Not started

## TIER 2: Studies That Need One Collaborator (Fellowship)

### Study 4: Pre-Operative Scapulothoracic Function Predicting Surgical Outcomes
- **Design:** Prospective cohort
- **Population:** Consecutive patients scheduled for cuff repair or rTSA
- **Measurements:** Pre-operative scapulothoracic assessment (SAT, visual dyskinesis, ideally wearable sensors). Post-operative ASES, Constant at 6-12 months.
- **Analysis:** Does pre-op scapulothoracic function predict post-op outcomes independently of surgical variables?
- **Collaborator needed:** Physical therapist in shoulder rehabilitation at fellowship institution
- **Why this matters:** If positive, this is the first study demonstrating that scapulothoracic function has prognostic value for shoulder surgery. Career-defining.
- **Timeline:** 6-12 months enrollment, 6-12 months follow-up
- **Status:** Not started. Collaborator not yet identified.

### Study 5: Scapular Prehab Pilot
- **Design:** Randomized pilot
- **Population:** Patients scheduled for rTSA
- **Intervention:** 6-12 weeks scapular-focused prehab (serratus anterior, lower trap) vs standard care
- **Outcomes:** ASES, Constant, SHR measurement if available, at 6-12 months
- **Collaborator needed:** Same PT collaborator as Study 4
- **Prerequisite:** Should ideally come after Study 4 results showing prediction
- **Timeline:** 12-18 months
- **Status:** Not started

### Study 6: Dynamic Glenoid Version Measurement
- **Design:** Cross-sectional kinematic study
- **Population:** Patients with B0/B1 glenoids vs matched controls with normal shoulders
- **Measurements:** Effective glenoid version during functional tasks via biplanar fluoroscopy
- **Question:** Do B0 patients have greater dynamic functional retroversion than static CT suggests?
- **Collaborator needed:** Motion analysis lab with biplanar fluoroscopy capability
- **Where to find:** University of Colorado biomechanics department, or nearby institution with shoulder kinematics research
- **Timeline:** 12-18 months
- **Status:** Not started. Lab not yet identified.

## TIER 3: Studies That Need Engineering Collaboration (Fellowship / Early Career)

### Study 7: Computational Tensegrity Model of the Scapulothoracic System
- **Design:** Computational modeling study
- **What it does:** Build a model with scapula as compression element, periscapular muscles as tension elements. Apply tensegrity equations. Compare model predictions to measured kinematic data.
- **Question:** Does a tensegrity model predict scapular kinematics accurately? Does it exhibit strain-hardening? Does computationally "weakening" serratus anterior produce the kinematics Ludewig 2000 measured?
- **Collaborator needed:** Biomedical engineer with musculoskeletal modeling expertise (OpenSim, AnyBody, or custom FE modeling)
- **Where to find:** BME department at University of Colorado, or reach out to the groups who built tensegrity shoulder robots (Baltaxe-Admony 2016, Li 2022)
- **How to approach:** Email with manuscript attached. "I have a clinical hypothesis about scapulothoracic biomechanics. I need engineering expertise to test it computationally. Would you be interested in collaborating?"
- **Timeline:** 12-24 months
- **Status:** Not started. Collaborator not yet identified.

### Study 8: CSA and Implant Selection in rTSA
- **Design:** Retrospective or prospective comparative study
- **Question:** Do rTSA outcomes differ by implant lateralization when stratified by pre-operative CSA or scapulothoracic orientation?
- **Collaborator needed:** High-volume arthroplasty surgeon with a database, potentially multi-center
- **Timeline:** 12-24 months depending on data availability
- **Status:** Not started

## INTELLECTUAL HERITAGE

This work stands on the shoulders of:

**The scapula matters:** Kibler (kinetic chain, 1998), Burkhart (SICK scapula, 2003), Ludewig (3D kinematics, 2000), McClure (bone pin kinematics, 2001)

**Scapular morphology predicts pathology:** Nyffeler (acromion index, 2006), Moor (CSA, 2013), Meyer (posterior acromial morphology, 2019), Gerber (biomechanical mechanism, 2014; surgical correction, 2018, 2025)

**Scapulothoracic orientation matters for arthroplasty:** Walker (SHR after rTSA, 2015), Kawashima (SHR predicts outcomes, 2025), Moroder (orientation affects component positioning, 2020), Shekhbihi (the scapulothoracic conundrum, 2025)

**The structural principle:** Ingber (cellular tensegrity, 1993-2014), Chen and Ingber (skeleton to cytoskeleton, 1999)

**The evolutionary context:** Kuhn (throwing and the shoulder, 2016), Selby (scapular evolution, 2017), Roach (elastic energy storage, Nature 2013)

**The sensitivity of the system:** Kebaetse (posture effects, 1999), Borstad (pec minor length effects, 2005), Seitz (SAT and subacromial space, 2012)

**The persistence of dysfunction:** Mercurio (dyskinesis after repair, 2026), Gibbons (sarcomere remodeling, 2016)

**The debate:** Arnar (CSA not associated with RCT, 2017), Vila-Dieguez (observer bias in dyskinesia assessment, 2026), Burn (prevalence in asymptomatic athletes, 2016)

This program does not claim to replace any of this work. It aims to connect it.
