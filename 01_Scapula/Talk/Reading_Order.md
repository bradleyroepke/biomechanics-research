## PHASE 1: THE FOUNDATION — What the Scapula Is (Read First)
*These papers establish the anatomy, biomechanics, and evolutionary context. Read these before anything else.*

### Evolutionary Context
1. ★ **Kuhn 2016** — Throwing, the Shoulder, and Human Evolution *(the evolutionary WHY — read this first, it reframes everything)*
2. ★ **Selby 2017** — Evolution of the hominoid scapula *(the two fundamental changes — narrowing + glenoid migration)*

### Foundational Anatomy & Biomechanics
3. ★ **Inman 1944** — Observations on the function of the shoulder joint *(THE classic — 2:1 SHR, the setting phase, "orthodox teaching is entirely incorrect")*
4. **Saha 1971** — Dynamic stability of the glenohumeral joint *(glenohumeral indices, dynamic stability concept)*
5. ★ **Poppen 1976** — Normal and abnormal motion of the shoulder *(refined SHR — ratio isn't fixed)*
6. **Poppen 1977** — Forces at the glenohumeral joint in abduction
7. **Culham 1993** — Functional anatomy of the shoulder complex *(atmospheric pressure stabilization, scapulothoracic mechanism)*
8. **Van der Helm 1994** — Finite element musculoskeletal model of the shoulder

### 3D Scapular Kinematics — The Modern Data
9. **Ludewig 1996** — Three-dimensional scapular orientation and muscle activity at selected positions
10. ★ **McClure 2001** — 3D scapular kinematics during movement *(bone pin study — the gold standard: SHR 1.7:1, 50° UR, 30° PT, 24° ER)*
11. **Van Andel 2007** — Complete 3D kinematics of upper extremity functional tasks
12. ★ **Ludewig 2009 JBJS** — Motion of the shoulder complex during multiplanar humeral elevation *(pin study, 3 elevation planes)*

### The Kinetic Chain
13. ★ **Kibler 1998** — The role of the scapula in athletic shoulder function *(51% kinetic energy, "feet off ground" quote, 5 roles of the scapula)*
14. **Sciascia 2012** — Kinetic chain rehabilitation: a theoretical framework
15. **Amansoof 2023** — Role of kinetic chain in sports performance and injury risk

## PHASE 2: THE NUMBERS — Why Small Changes Matter
*These papers quantify how sensitive the scapulothoracic system is to perturbation.*

16. ★ **Kebaetse 1999** — Thoracic position effect on shoulder ROM, strength, and 3D scapular kinematics *(23.6° less abduction, 16.2% less force from slouching)*
17. ★ **Borstad 2005** — Effect of pectoralis minor resting length on scapular kinematics *(4.1mm → 1.4mm subacromial space; short pec minor → altered kinematics)*
18. ★ **Seitz 2012** — SAT results in changes in scapular position and subacromial space *(the actionable clinical test)*

## PHASE 3: THREAD 1 SUPPORT — Altered Kinematics in Pathology
*These papers show what happens when the scapula moves wrong.*

19. ★ **Ludewig 2000** — Alterations in shoulder kinematics in people with impingement *(THE landmark: decreased UR, decreased PT, increased IR, decreased serratus activity)*
20. **Lukasiewicz 1999** — 3D scapular position with and without impingement *(confirmed altered position)*
21. ★ **McClure 2006** — Shoulder function and 3D kinematics with and without impingement *(NUANCED — found MORE upward rotation in impingement, possible compensation)*
22. ★ **Ludewig 2009 JOSPT** — The association of scapular kinematics and GH joint pathologies *(comprehensive review linking kinematics to multiple pathologies)*
23. **Michener 2013** — Anatomical and biomechanical mechanisms of subacromial impingement

## PHASE 4: THREAD 2 — Shape Predicts Pathology (CSA)
*Read these in chronological order to follow the intellectual lineage.*

24. ★ **Nyffeler 2006** — Association of a large lateral extension of the acromion with rotator cuff tears *(384 cites — the Acromion Index, precursor to CSA)*
25. ★ **Moor 2013** — A radiological study of the critical shoulder angle *(THE CSA paper: 33.1°/38.0°/28.1°, AUC 0.92)*
26. ★ **Viehöfer 2016** — A larger CSA requires more rotator cuff activity *(the mechanism — 23% more shear, forces exceed physiological values)*
27. ★ **Davide 2016** — Predominance of the CSA in degenerative diseases *(dose-response: 34°/36°/40°, OR 1.7)*
28. **Mantell 2017** — CSA is associated with full-thickness RCT in patients with OA *(90% specific, 52% sensitive)*
29. **Arnar 2017** — CSA is associated with OA but not rotator cuff tears *(THE contradicting paper — OR 1.12 for RCT, OR 2.25 for OA)*
30. ★ **Garcia 2017** — Higher CSA increases risk of retear after cuff repair *(OR 14.8 for retear if CSA >38°)*
31. **Lee 2017** — CSA does not influence 24-month outcome after cuff repair *(another contradicting paper)*
32. ★ **Gerber 2018** — Arthroscopic correction of CSA through lateral acromioplasty *(37.5° → 33.9°, 25% more strength, safe, no deltoid compromise)*
33. **Li 2018** — Large CSA has higher risk of tendon retear
34. **Docter 2019** — CSA and RCT outcomes: systematic review
35. **Katthagen 2016** — Effects of arthroscopic lateral acromioplasty: cadaveric study
36. ★ **Passaplan 2021** — CSA does not change over time *(10+ year follow-up — innate, not acquired)*

## PHASE 5: THREAD 2 — Shape Predicts Pathology (Posterior Acromion)
*The Gerber group's posterior instability story.*

37. ★ **Meyer 2019** — Posterior acromial morphology is significantly associated with posterior shoulder instability *(OR 39, PAH 30.9 vs 19.5mm, "Swiss chalet roof")*
38. ★ **Hochreiter 2023** — Posterior stability depends on acromial anatomy: 3D biomechanical study *(glenoid alone insufficient — must correct acromion)*
39. ★ **Gerber 2023** — Correction of static posterior shoulder subluxation using acromion and glenoid osteotomies *(the original SCOPE case report)*
40. ★ **Gerber 2025** — Scapular osteotomies for posterior shoulder instability: technique and preliminary results *(n=9, 6/9 revisions, SSV +42.5%)*
